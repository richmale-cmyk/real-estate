The decision that caused the incident was usually made weeks earlier.

In a budget meeting. In a staffing call. In a quiet conversation that ended with "we'll manage."

By the time something goes visibly wrong, the causal chain is long and the real decision points are buried. What looks like an operational failure is usually a series of small compromises that accumulated until the system had no resilience left.

This is why post-incident reviews frustrate me. We analyse the moment of failure. We rarely go back far enough to find the moment of choice.

The question I ask more now: *what decision made today will I regret in three months?*

Not what's on fire. What quiet thing am I letting slide that will matter later.
